Low-Power Cardiac Arrhythmia Detection on ESP32-S3 (SNN vs ANN)

Implementing and evaluating a low-power, real-time cardiac arrhythmia detection system on ESP32-S3 microcontroller. Comparing Spiking Neural Network (SNN) performance against conventional ANN baselines in terms of accuracy, latency, memory footprint, and energy consumption for medical edge computing applications.

Research Impact

Advances neuromorphic computing for medical devices and establishes benchmarks for SNN efficiency on microcontrollers.

Continual Learning Image Classifier on ESP32-S3

A multi-class image classifier deployed on ESP32-S3 with OV2640 camera using quantized MobileNetV2 via TensorFlow Lite Micro. The project explores continuous learning on-device, enabling the classifier to expand to new classes over time while operating within strict memory and energy constraints of edge devices.

Technical Innovation

Implements on-device learning capabilities on microcontroller hardware, pushing boundaries of edge AI adaptability.
