About Me

AI researcher and embedded systems engineer specializing in efficient neural network deployment for edge computing applications.

My Focus & Philosophy

I am a student with a deep interest in artificial intelligence, neural networks, and embedded machine learning. Instead of focusing on traditional ML models, I specialize in ANNs and SNNs, exploring how intelligent models can run efficiently on resource-constrained devices.

My work centers around deploying and evaluating models on microcontrollers such as the ESP32-S3. I focus on practical metrics like accuracy, latency, memory footprint, and energy consumption, particularly for applications like cardiac arrhythmia detection.

I'm also exploring continuous / lifelong learning on edge devices—enabling AI models to learn new tasks over time without extensive retraining. My ultimate goal is to build robust, energy-efficient AI systems that operate effectively in real-world environments.
